Sea Level Analysis

Interactive map · NOAA tide-gauge data
Node.jsExpress LeafletNOAA API Render

An interactive map that pulls real relative-sea-level records from NOAA tide-gauge stations, draws the long-run trend at each one, and projects it forward. The point isn't a scary headline number — it's letting you stand at a specific harbour and see what a century of monthly water-level readings actually says about that place.

Below is the method the station trends rest on.

The math behind a station's trend

Each station gives a monthly mean sea level (MSL) series. To recover the long-run rate I fit a straight line by ordinary least squares:

MSL(t) = β₀ + β₁·t + ε

β₁ is the trend — millimetres per year — and β₀ the intercept. Before fitting, the seasonal swing is removed by regressing out annual and semi-annual harmonics (sine/cosine terms at 12- and 6-month periods), so a warm-summer bulge doesn't masquerade as a trend.

The catch: sea level is strongly autocorrelated month to month, so the naïve OLS standard error on β₁ is far too small. The reported uncertainty widens the standard error to account for that serial correlation, giving an honest confidence interval on the rate rather than a falsely precise one. Projection is then extrapolation of the fitted line over the chosen horizon.

Troo News

Multilingual news aggregator
Express43 outlets 7 languagesBias indicators

A news aggregator for the Indian press, built in the spirit of Ground News: take a single event, gather how it's being told across 43 outlets in seven languages, and put those versions side by side with a lean indicator on each. The interesting problem isn't scraping — it's recognising that a Hindi headline and an English one are about the same story, then making the difference in framing something a reader can see at a glance instead of guessing at.

Research

R1

VACP

Vocabulary-Aware Conformal Prediction for LLMs
Conformal predictionLLMs Uncertainty

Conformal prediction wraps a model's output in a set that's guaranteed to contain the truth with a chosen probability. For a language model that's lovely in theory and useless in practice — the guaranteed set sprawls across most of the vocabulary. VACP uses the structure of the vocabulary itself to cut the prediction set dramatically while keeping the coverage guarantee intact.

197× smaller prediction sets — coverage preserved
R2

Drishti — current

Calibrated political-bias estimation
LaBSEHDBSCAN MuRILMondrian conformal

What I'm building now: a pipeline that estimates political lean in Indian media and — crucially — attaches a calibrated confidence to every estimate instead of a bare label. LaBSE embeddings align the same story across languages, HDBSCAN clusters reporting into events, a MuRIL-based classifier reads the framing, and Mondrian conformal calibration makes the confidence valid within groups, not just on average.

The full codebase is in place and currently awaiting hand labels for validation.
